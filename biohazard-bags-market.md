<p><p>The biohazard bags market encompasses various materials, including LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene), HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene), cellophane, and polypropylene. LDPE bags are flexible and puncture-resistant, making them ideal for safe disposal. HDPE bags are stronger and more tear-resistant, suitable for sharp objects. Cellophane offers a biodegradable option but lacks strength. Polypropylene bags are durable and resistant to chemicals, often used in healthcare settings. Each type serves specific needs in handling and disposing of biohazardous materials safely.</p></p>
