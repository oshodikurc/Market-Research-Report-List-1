<p><ul><li>Wet Heat Bain Maries</li><li>Dry Heat Bain Maries</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bain Maries are categorized into wet heat and dry heat types, each serving distinct purposes in food service. Wet heat Bain Maries use water to maintain food temperatures, suitable for soups and sauces, ensuring even heating and moisture retention. Dry heat Bain Maries, on the other hand, use convection to keep foods warm without added moisture, ideal for cooked meats and casseroles, preventing sogginess. Both types are essential in commercial kitchens for food safety and quality maintenance.</p></p>
