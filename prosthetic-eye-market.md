<p><p>The prosthetic eye market is categorized primarily into two types: non-integrated implants and integrated implants. Non-integrated implants are standalone devices that mimic the appearance of a natural eye but do not interact functionally with existing eye structures. In contrast, integrated implants are designed to connect with the body's biological systems, often incorporating advanced technologies to restore vision or mimic eye movements. Both types address unique patient needs, with innovations driving advancements in prosthetic design and functionality.</p></p>
