<p><strong>The Military Airborne Collision Avoidance Systems Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Radar</li><li>TCAS</li><li>PCAS</li><li>FLARM</li><li>GPWS</li><li>TAWS</li><li>Synthetic Vision</li><li>OCAS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Military airborne collision avoidance systems encompass various technologies designed to enhance flight safety. Radar systems detect potential collisions by tracking nearby aircraft. TCAS (Traffic Alert and Collision Avoidance System) provides alerts and resolution advisories to pilots. PCAS (Passive Collision Avoidance System) analyzes transponder signals for situational awareness. FLARM is used in general aviation to prevent collisions with gliders. GPWS (Ground Proximity Warning System) and TAWS (Terrain Awareness and Warning System) help prevent ground impact. Synthetic Vision offers a 3D view of the environment, while OCAS (Obstacle Collision Avoidance System) identifies ground-level obstacles. Each system plays a crucial role in maintaining safe operations.</p></p>
