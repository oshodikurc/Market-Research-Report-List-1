<p><p>The Virtual Reality (VR) software market can be divided into two main types: on-premise and cloud. On-premise VR software is installed locally on a user's hardware, offering greater control and customization but requiring significant infrastructure. Conversely, cloud-based VR solutions leverage remote servers for processing and storage, enabling easier accessibility and scalability without high upfront costs. Both types cater to different user needs, balancing between control versus convenience and cost-efficiency in deploying immersive VR experiences.</p></p>
