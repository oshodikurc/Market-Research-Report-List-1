<p><p>The bioelectronic medicine market encompasses devices such as pacemakers, cochlear implants, and spinal cord stimulators. Pacemakers regulate heart rhythms, enhancing cardiac function. Cochlear implants provide auditory input for individuals with hearing loss, converting sound into electrical signals for the brain. Spinal cord stimulators alleviate chronic pain by delivering electrical impulses to the spinal cord, disrupting pain signals. Collectively, these technologies demonstrate the potential of bioelectronic devices to improve patient outcomes and enhance quality of life across various medical conditions.</p></p>
