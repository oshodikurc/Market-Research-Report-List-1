<p><p>The trauma fixation equipment market comprises various internal and external fixation devices designed to stabilize fractured bones. Internal fixation includes plates, screws, nails, and other devices that are surgically implanted to support bone healing. Plates provide broad support, screws secure bone ends, and nails implant within the bone shaft. External fixation involves devices applied outside the body to stabilize fractures, offering adjustable support while minimizing soft tissue damage. Each type serves distinct purposes based on fracture specifics and treatment strategies.</p></p>
