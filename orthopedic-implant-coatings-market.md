<p><p>The orthopedic implant coatings market features several types, including sintered bead coatings, plasma sprayed metallic coatings, and hydroxyapatite (HA) coatings. Sintered bead coatings provide a rough surface for better bone integration, enhancing stability. Plasma sprayed metallic coatings, typically made of titanium or cobalt-chromium, offer increased corrosion resistance and mechanical strength. Hydroxyapatite coatings mimic bone mineral composition, promoting osteoconduction and facilitating faster healing. Each coating type serves distinct functional roles to improve implant performance and longevity.</p></p>
